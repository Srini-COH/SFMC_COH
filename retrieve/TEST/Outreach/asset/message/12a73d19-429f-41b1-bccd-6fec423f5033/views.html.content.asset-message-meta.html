As a National Cancer Institute-designated comprehensive cancer center, City of Hope® is dedicated to advancing care for lung cancer patients through leading-edge technologies and innovative clinical trials, while also caring for the whole patient with personalized supportive care services. Named one of America’s top 10 “Best Hospitals” for cancer and ranked “high performing” in lung cancer surgery, among other treatments, by U.S. News & World Report, City of Hope provides exceptional support from diagnosis through survivorship, delivered by a multidisciplinary team of specialists who work together to provide a patient-centered approach to treat lung cancer at every stage of disease.

Epidermal Growth Factor Receptor (EGFR) Mutated Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer
NCT05166616:

Principal investigator Erminia Massarelli, M.D., Ph.D., M.S., is actively recruiting patients for a study on the benefits and best dose of Minnelide when given together with osimertinib for the treatment of non-small cell lung cancer that has spread to other places in the body and has the EGFR gene mutation.
Patient Enrollment Qualifications

Qualifying patients must:
  • Be 18 years of age or older
  • Have histologically confirmed advanced non-small cell lung cancer
  • Have a tumor that harbors one of the two common EGFR mutations
  • Have tumor progression after receiving standard/approved osimertinib
ALK-Rearranged Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer
NCT04227028:

Principal investigator Victoria Meucci Villaflor, M.D., is actively recruiting patients for a study on the benefits of brigatinib plus bevacizumab in patients with ALK-rearranged non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) that has progressed after receiving ALK-directed therapy.
Patient Enrollment Qualifications

Qualifying patients must:
  • Be 18 years of age or older
  • Have locally advanced, recurrent or metastatic ALK-rearranged NSCLC
  • Have previous progression on ALK inhibitor
  • Have adequate organ and bone marrow function
Untreated Extensive-Stage Small Cell Lung Carcinoma
NCT04560972:

Principal investigator Ravi Salgia, M.D., Ph.D., is actively recruiting patients for a trial on the benefits of LB-100 given in combination with carboplatin, etoposide and atezolizumab in patients with untreated extensive-stage small cell carcinoma.
Patient Enrollment Qualifications

Qualifying patients must:
  • Be 18 years of age or older
  • Have extensive-stage small cell lung cancer
  • Have not had prior treatment
Oligoprogressive Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer, Melanoma and Renal Cell Carcinoma
NCT05830058:

Principal investigator Arya Amini, M.D., is recruiting patients for a study evaluating the safety and efficacy of positron emission tomography (PET)-guided stereotactic body radiation therapy (SBRT), as compared to standard SBRT, to treat non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC), melanoma and renal cell carcinoma that has up to five sites of small metastatic lesions.
Patient Enrollment Qualifications

Qualifying patients must:
  • Be 18 years of age or older
  • Have been diagnosed with NSCLC with 1-5 sites of disease progression
  • Be on have received systemic therapy with a checkpoint inhibitor with or without chemotherapy for at least three months
