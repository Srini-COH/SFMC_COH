As a National Cancer Institute-designated comprehensive cancer center, City of Hope is ranked as one of the nation’s top 10 “Best Hospitals” for cancer in the nation by U.S. News & World Report, in addition to being recognized as high performing in breast cancer surgery.

City of Hope offers breast cancer patients at all stages world-class care from a multidisciplinary team of experts who take an integrated approach to diagnose and treat breast cancer. As a cancer center whose primary focus is cancer treatment and care, City of Hope continues to treat thousands of patients a year, with extensive experience in robotic surgery and leading-edge treatments. Additionally, breast cancer patients at City of Hope also have access to a variety of clinical trials, evaluating promising treatments and therapies focusing on improving patient outcomes.

At City of Hope, when you refer a patient, our team of experts and health care professionals are committed to providing whole-patient care and world-class resources for all cancers. We accept a wide range of insurance plans for our patients, including Medi-Cal.

NCT04660929:

Principal investigator Joanne Mortimer, M.D., is actively recruiting participants for a Phase 1, first-in-human, open-label trial to study the effectiveness of adenovirally transduced autologous macrophages engineered to contain an anti-HER2 chimeric antigen receptor in patients with HER2-overexpressing solid tumors.
Patient Enrollment Qualifications

Patients must:
  • Be 18 years of age or older
  • Have HER2-positive recurrent or metastatic solid tumors for which no available treatment options are available
  • Be willing to undergo biopsy procedures
  • Not have HIV or hepatitis B or C
  • Not have an active autoimmune disease that required systemic treatment in the previous two years
NCT04020575:

Principal investigator Joanne Mortimer, M.D., is actively recruiting for a Phase 1/2 trial to study adoptive immunotherapy for advanced MUC1-positive breast cancer with autologous T cells engineered to express multiple varieties of chimeric antigen receptors that are specific for a cleaved form of MUC1.
Patient Enrollment Qualifications

Patients must:
  • Be 18 years of age or older
  • Have a confirmed diagnosis of breast cancer by a pathology review of a biopsy or other material at
    City of Hope
  • Have received standard systemic therapy for metastatic disease
  • Not have an active autoimmune disease requiring immunosuppression therapy
  • Not have major organ dysfunction
NCT05081492:

Principal investigator Jamie Rand, M.D., is actively recruiting for a Phase 1, first-in-human trial to study the safety, dosage levels and potential side effects of the oncolytic virus CF33-hNIS-antiPDL1 in patients with metastatic triple-negative breast cancer.
Patient Enrollment Qualifications

Patients must:
  • Be 18 years of age or older
  • Have a histologically confirmed diagnosis of metastatic triple-negative breast cancer
  • Have had their cancer progress or been intolerant of at least two previous lines of treatment
  • Not have received chemotherapy, biologic therapy, immunotherapy or investigational therapy in the two weeks prior to Day 1 of the trial
NCT03696030:

Principal investigator Jana Portnow, M.D., is actively recruiting for a Phase 1 trial to study the dosage levels and potential side effects of HER2-CAR T cells in patients with recurrent cancer that has spread to the brain or leptomeninges.
Patient Enrollment Qualifications

Patients must:
  • Be 18 to 75 years of age
  • Have a life expectancy of more than eight weeks
  • Have recurrent brain metastases after radiation therapy, or recurrent leptomeningeal metastases after intrathecal chemotherapy or have untreated brain or leptomeningeal metastases and have refused to undergo radiation therapy and/or intrathecal chemotherapy
