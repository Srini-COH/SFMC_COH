As a National Cancer Institute-designated comprehensive cancer center focused on developing novel ways to treat and care for patients with cancer, City of Hope’s Lung Cancer and Thoracic Oncology Program is uniquely positioned to provide personalized treatment to transform the future of lung health.
Our clinical care network offers patients access to innovative approaches, treatments and benefits to meet each patient’s needs, including:
• Cost-effective, low-dose computed tomography (CT) scanning technology
• Robotic-assisted endoscopy technology that enables bronchoscopic visualization and lung cancer diagnosis at Stage 1 or 2
• Advanced diagnostic and therapeutic interventions, including robot-assisted bronchoscopy
• Investigational diagnostic testing, offered through enrollment in clinical trials
• Precision medicine, such as targeted therapy, chimeric antigen receptor (CAR) T cell therapy and immunotherapy
• Molecular and genetic tests tailor treatment to each patient
• Exceptional supportive care services and survivorship resources
• Smoking Cessation Program

Our Leading-Edge Surgical Capabilities Improve Lung Cancer Treatment and Prognosis

U.S. News & World Report has named City of Hope a high-performing hospital for lung cancer surgery in part because City of Hope’s Division of Thoracic Surgery employs the latest and most effective techniques, including robotic-assisted surgery, VATS (video-assisted thoracoscopic surgery) and thoracotomy.

At City of Hope, our surgical team includes highly trained thoracic specialists who regularly take on complex cases — including some deemed untreatable at other facilities — and are among the best when it comes to high-quality, minimally invasive lung-sparing thoracic surgery. About City of Hope
City of Hope's mission is to deliver the cures of tomorrow to the people who need them today. Founded in 1913, City of Hope has grown into one of the largest cancer research and treatment organizations in the U.S. and one of the leading research centers for diabetes and other life-threatening illnesses. City of Hope research has been the basis for numerous breakthrough cancer medicines, as well as human synthetic insulin and monoclonal antibodies. With an independent, National Cancer Institute-designated comprehensive cancer center at its core, City of Hope brings a uniquely integrated model to patients spanning cancer care, research and development, academics and training, and innovation initiatives. City of Hope’s growing national system includes its Los Angeles campus, a network of clinical care locations across Southern California, a new cancer center in Orange County, California, and treatment facilities in Atlanta, Chicago and Phoenix. City of Hope’s affiliated group of organizations includes Translational Genomics Research Institute and AccessHopeTM.
