As a director of gastroenterology services, I am committed to improving the quality of life for patients with gastrointestinal malignancies. As part of this commitment, I am proud to share that my colleagues and I have expanded our interventional gastroenterology offerings and now offer biliary radiofrequency ablation (RFA).

Biliary RFA may be used in patients who are not candidates for chemotherapy or surgical management, or who have disease progression on chemotherapy. This safe and minimally invasive approach uses bipolar energy to partially or completely ablate malignant and benign tissue in the biliary tract, and to clear obstructed stents. Biliary RFA used prior to stent insertion can delay tumor growth, thereby improving patency of stents. It has the capability to improve patients’ quality of life and maintain disease control.

If you feel one of your patients would be a candidate for biliary RFA, please feel free to contact me through this forum. I am practicing at Cancer Treatment Centers of America (CTCA), Tulsa. In addition, we are equipped to offer several other interventional gastroenterology procedures including photodynamic therapy (PDT), cryotherapy, small bowel enteroscopy and esophageal stenting.
