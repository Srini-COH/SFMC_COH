As the Medical Director of the Lung Center at Cancer Treatment Centers of America® (CTCA) in Tulsa, I am focused on how to best fight and treat lung cancer. Studies* have shown that screening high-risk individuals with low-dose CT scans could reduce lung cancer mortality by 20 percent, so I’m pleased to share that the CTCA® facility in Tulsa is now offering services for lung cancer screening and diagnosis.
 
The U.S. Preventative Task Force recommends annual screening for lung cancer with a low-dose CT scan for the following patients:
  • 55 to 80 years old
  • 30+ pack-year smoking history
  • Currently smoke or have quit within the past 15 years
 
Our program offers:
  • Quick Appointments – Patients are typically seen the same week they call for an appointment.
  • Rapid Results – Screening results are delivered to you and your patient quickly, often within 48 hours.
  • Access to Specialists – If your patient needs additional follow-up, CTCA offers quick access to a multidisciplinary team of board-certified interventional radiologists and pulmonologists as well as thoracic surgeons to diagnose and manage any aspect of thoracic malignancy.
  • Patient Follow-up – Our screening software system helps ensure patients don’t miss crucial follow-up appointments and that you are notified of the latest with their care.
