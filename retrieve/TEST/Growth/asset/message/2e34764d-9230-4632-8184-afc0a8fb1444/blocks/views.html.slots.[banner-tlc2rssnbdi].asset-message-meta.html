I want to personally share with you an upcoming CME opportunity in Tulsa, OK.
 
The management of cancer patients is complex and requires the coordination of many clinical experts across a variety of disciplines. Further, new treatments and palliative methods are constantly emerging, bringing with them numerous side effects and interactions. Cancer Treatment Centers of America (CTCA), in partnership with the Academy for Continued Healthcare Learning and the Osteopathic Founders Foundation, is offering a free, live CME event at Oklahoma State University Health Science Center to discuss this topic on March 9, 2019 at 7:30 AM. I will be presenting, along with my colleagues from CTCA and MD Anderson.
 
The fourth annual Advancements in Cancer Management will again address the most important topics affecting the management of cancer patients by general practitioners, highlighting the newest data and research. This series is consistently recognized by attendees as valuable education, with 99% of last years’ attendees saying they would recommend the course to a colleague and that all learning objectives were achieved.
 
Upon completion of this CME event, the attendee will be able to:
  • Explain the use of multimodal analgesia to manage pain and reduce the use of opioids
  • Describe how integrative therapies and lifestyle change programs are employed as adjunct therapies in the care of cancer patients
  • Evaluate immuno-oncology approaches in cancer treatment, including patient selection
  • Describe the appropriate follow-up protocols for identification of pulmonary nodules
  • Recognize the pathophysiology and clinical presentations of various subtypes of pleural effusions
  • Describe the various treatment options for each of the various types of pleural effusions
I hope to see you at this event. Feel free to share this invitation with others you feel may benefit from learning more about the management of cancer patients and participating in this educational program.
