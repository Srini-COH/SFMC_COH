From donating to receiving: What you need to know about stem cell transplants

When cancer develops in the blood, it usually doesn’t form tumors. Instead, it damages blood cells that quickly multiply, crowd out healthy cells and overtake the bloodstream. When that happens, many oncologists will consider a stem cell transplant to replace the damaged blood cells with healthy new ones. Undergoing a stem cell transplant is like giving new life to the blood in patients diagnosed with a liquid cancer, such as leukemia, lymphoma and multiple myeloma.
