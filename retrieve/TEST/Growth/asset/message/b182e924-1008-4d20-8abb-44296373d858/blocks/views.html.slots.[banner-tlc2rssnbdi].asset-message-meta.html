It is estimated that 80% of cancer patients experience disease-related pain.

When not managed, cancer pain can adversely affect treatment outcomes and quality of life, including additional hospitalizations, increased health care costs, as well as lack of adherence to and/or delayed treatment.

As a board certified and fellowship-trained pain management physician with expertise in treating cancer patients, I can be a resource to you for your patients struggling with cancer-related pain.

In my practice at Cancer Treatment Centers of America, Atlanta, we provide care for episodic and advanced cancer-related pain utilizing a multimodal approach and interventions through both pharmaceutical and non-pharmaceutical approaches as appropriate, including minimally invasive procedures such as:
  • Bone tumor ablation
  • Vertebra augmentation
  • Systematic nerve blocks
  • Neuromodulation
  • Musculoskeletal injections
  • Facet joint injections
  • Radiofrequency ablation/thermoneurolysis/denervation
  • Peripheral nerve blocks
  • Joint injections
  • Bursa injections
When opioid medications are necessary, we closely monitor patients for signs of dependency and rapidly respond to alternatives as needed.

Recognizing the urgency of effectively treating patients’ pain, our team has made it a priority to rapidly schedule patients, sometimes seeing them within three days of referral. If you send a patient to us, please know that we are committed to HIPAA-compliant communications with you as the patient’s primary oncology provider to assist with continuity of care.
