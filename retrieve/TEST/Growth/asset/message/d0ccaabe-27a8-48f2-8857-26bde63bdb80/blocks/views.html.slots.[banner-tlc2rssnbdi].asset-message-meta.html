It is estimated that 80% of cancer patients experience disease-related pain. In my practice, I have primarily managed cancer patients’ pain with evidence-based interventions. However, with a goal to improve the lives of all patients, my colleague, George Macrinici, MD and I have recently expanded our services to include all patients with back pain, regardless of a malignant diagnosis.

Both Dr. Macrinici and I are fellowship trained and board certified in pain management. We perform a variety of interventional pain management techniques that focus on improving quality of life utilizing both pharmacologic and nonpharmacologic interventions. Some of the minimally-invasive procedures we offer include:
  • Radiofrequency ablation: implant-free procedure that uses bipolar radiofrequency energy to ablate the basivertebral nerve for chronic low back pain
  • Pain signal-blocking procedures (including dorsal root ganglion, spinal cord and peripheral nerve stimulation): placement of a stimulator to disrupt pain signals to the brain
  • Intrathecal baclofen therapy (ITB): implantation of a pump to deliver continuous doses of baclofen directly into the spinal fluid, eliminating the need for oral baclofen for spinal cord injury patients
Dr. Macrinici and I practice at Cancer Treatment Centers of America (CTCA) in Zion, IL, which is located midway between Chicago and Milwaukee. We are committed to rapidly scheduling appointments upon referral, keeping referring physicians updated through HIPAA-compliant communications, and appropriately transitioning patients back into their care once a procedure is complete.

If you have patients who have failed other chronic back pain treatments that you feel would benefit from one of these options, we would be happy to talk with you further.
