As the Director of Interventional Pulmonology at Cancer Treatment Centers of America (CTCA) in Suburban Chicago, I am focused on how to best fight lung cancer. Studies* have shown that screening high-risk individuals with low-dose CT scans could reduce lung cancer mortality by 20 percent, so I’m pleased to share that the CTCA facility in Chicago offers services for lung cancer screening and diagnosis.
 
The U.S. Preventative Task Force recommends annual screening for lung cancer with a low-dose CT scan for the following patients:
  • 55 to 80 years old
  • 30+ pack-year smoking history
  • Currently smoke or have quit within the past 15 years
Our program offers:
  • Quick Appointments – Patients are typically seen the same week they call for an appointment.
  • Rapid Results – Screening results are delivered to you and your patient quickly, often within 48 hours.
  • Patient Follow-up – Our screening software system helps ensure patients don’t miss crucial follow-up appointments and that you are notified of the latest with their care.
  • Access to Specialists – If your patient needs additional follow-up, we offer quick access to a multidisciplinary team of board-certified interventional radiologists and pulmonologists as well as thoracic surgeons and medical oncologists to diagnose and manage any aspect of thoracic malignancy.
  • Fast Diagnosis – We are equipped to provide safe, minimally-invasive procedures, such as endobronchial ultrasound (EBUS) with electromagnetic navigation (ENB) bronchoscopy, to biopsy suspicious nodules, providing a diagnosis in as little as 72 hours.
