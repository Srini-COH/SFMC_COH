COVID-19 subvariants posing new threat to cancer patients

Everywhere you go, it seems like the pre-pandemic good ol’ days. With mask mandates and other restrictions lifted in many places, the hand-sanitized, surgical-masked days of social distancing seem like a thing of the past. But if you’re a cancer patient, caregiver or family member, you may not want to let your guard down just yet. COVID-19 still poses a threat to cancer patients and others who have compromised immune systems. And a recently discovered subvariant, which is the dominant strain among new cases, is proving to be highly contagious.
