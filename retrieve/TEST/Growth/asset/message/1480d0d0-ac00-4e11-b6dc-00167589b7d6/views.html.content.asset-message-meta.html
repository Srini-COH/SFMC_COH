Why men get cancer more than women and how they can manage their risk

Doctors have known for decades that men are more likely to develop cancer than women. Men have a one-in-two chance of being diagnosed with cancer during their lifetimes; for women, the chance is one in three. For decades, no one could pinpoint the cause for the disparity. Scientists now believe they can account for the gender imbalance in some common cancers. Learn the specific steps men can take to make healthy lifestyle changes that may reduce their cancer risks.
