I want to personally share with you an upcoming multidisciplinary CME event for primary care and specialists providing care to patients with cancer.  This no cost event will be held in Rosemont, IL.
 
The management of cancer patients, specifically the medical sequelae of their treatment and disease, is increasingly complex requiring the collaboration of clinical experts across a variety of disciplines. Promising new treatments are rapidly emerging, bringing with them new side effects and interactions with many common organ systems typically managed by other specialties. You deserve to be better informed about these new treatments, and the management of their side effects.  There are also tremendous advances in pain management and in the complications of cancer that you should hear about.  Cancer Treatment Centers of America (CTCA), in partnership with the Academy for Continued Healthcare Learning and the Osteopathic Founders Foundation, is offering a free, live CME event at Gene and Georgetti, Rosemont, to discuss these topics on May 4, 2019 at 7:30 a.m. My colleagues from CTCA and The University of Texas MD Anderson will be presenting.
 
The fourth annual Advancements in Cancer Management will again address the most important topics affecting the management of cancer patients by general practitioners, highlighting the newest data and research. This series is consistently recognized by attendees as valuable education, with 99% of last years’ attendees saying they would recommend the course to a colleague and that all learning objectives were achieved.
 
Upon completion of this CME event, the attendee will be able to:
  • Evaluate immuno-oncology approaches in cancer treatment, including patient selection and side effect management.
  • Explain the use of multimodal analgesia to manage pain and reduce the use of opioids in cancer patients.
  • Describe how integrative therapies and lifestyle change programs are employed as adjunct therapies in the care of cancer patients
  • Describe the appropriate follow-up protocols for identification of pulmonary nodules
  • Recognize the pathophysiology and clinical presentations of various subtypes of pleural effusions and their management.
I hope to see you at this event. Feel free to share this invitation with others you feel may benefit from learning more about the management of cancer patients and participating in this educational program.
