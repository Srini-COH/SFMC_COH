Far less aggressive approaches are gaining ground for some cancers

Cancer was once viewed as a disease that could only be treated through total destruction. Doctors—and patients—believed that the only way to get rid of cancer was to treat it swiftly and comprehensively, even if it meant negatively affecting the patient in the process. “Getting rid of the cancer was always our first priority, no matter the cost,” says Peter Baik, DO, FACOS, Thoracic Surgery Director at CTCA Lung Cancer Institute. “Anything that was cancerous, we wanted to attack, so we generally chose the most aggressive approach possible.”
