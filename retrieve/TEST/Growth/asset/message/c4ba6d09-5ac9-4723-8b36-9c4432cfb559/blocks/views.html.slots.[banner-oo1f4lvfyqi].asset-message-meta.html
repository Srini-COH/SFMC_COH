Tennis legend: My sister’s death from cancer ‘saved my life’

Tennis pros Chris and Jeanne Evert were as close as sisters can be, but it was Jeanne Evert’s death from ovarian cancer in 2020 at the age of 62 that permanently bound the two sisters together in a way Chris Evert could never imagine. “My sister’s death saved my life,” she said in a recent interview in which she opened up about her own battle with cancer and how Jeanne’s genetic test led to her diagnosis years later. The Everts’ case also underscores the potential importance of genetic counseling and testing, especially when a relative is diagnosed with a cancer, such as breast or ovarian, that may be linked to inherited mutations.
