I am connecting with you to offer treatment options for Barrett’s esophagus. I have practiced in the Chicago area for nearly three decades and specialize in the diagnosis and treatment of the condition, which in select patient populations, can become malignant. As part of our cancer prevention efforts, the Cancer Treatment Centers of America (CTCA) interventional gastroenterology team in suburban Chicago developed a comprehensive treatment program for Barrett’s esophagus.
 
In recent years, several guidelines published by the American College of Gastroenterology recommend endoscopic ablative therapy for patients with Barrett’s esophagus, high-grade dysplasia and T1a esophageal adenocarcinoma.*  An example of this evolution in approach to care is the recent approval of cryotherapy for low- and high-grade dysplasia. Additionally, radiofrequency ablation (RFA) and endoscopic mucosal resection (EMR) are also approved ablation options. At CTCA, we offer all three approaches with a commitment to:
  • Quick appointments – Patients are typically seen the same week they are referred for an appointment.
  • Thorough evaluation – Dependent upon previous tests conducted at your office, patients will likely be seen for a one-day evaluation that includes an endoscopy and biopsy.
  • Prompt treatment – If appropriate for treatment, patients who are pre-certified will be scheduled for an ablative therapy no later than 30 days following the evaluation.
  • Strong communication – I commit to keeping you updated on your referred patient’s status via HIPAA-compliant communication throughout the evaluation process and treatment. Once treatment is complete, I will work with you to appropriately transition patients back into your care.
