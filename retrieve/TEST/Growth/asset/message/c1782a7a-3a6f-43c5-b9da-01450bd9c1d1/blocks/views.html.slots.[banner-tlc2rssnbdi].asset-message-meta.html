The National Cancer Institute predicts that breast cancer incident rates will rise 50 percent by 2030. In response, my colleagues at Cancer Treatment Centers of America (CTCA), Phoenix and I have developed a multidisciplinary high-risk breast cancer program.
 
Patients who are considered at higher risk for developing breast cancer include those with:
  • A first-degree relative diagnosed with breast or ovarian cancer
  • A family history with multiple members diagnosed with breast, ovarian, prostate and/or pancreatic cancer
  • An abnormal finding after a breast biopsy, particularly atypical ductal hyperplasia (ADH), lobular carcinoma in situ (LCIS) or atypical lobular hyperplasia (ALH)
  • A history of carcinogen exposure (e.g., previous radiation therapy)
  • A known genetic mutation or risk factors which may predispose them to a genetic mutation

At CTCA Phoenix, we provide a comprehensive clinical program including risk assessment, diagnostics, genetic counseling and testing, surgical risk-reduction strategies, prevention guidance and active surveillance. Our team is committed to rapidly scheduling appointments, often seeing patients within a week of referral. We are also committed to keep referring physicians updated through HIPAA-compliant communications on their patients’ status, as well as appropriately and deliberately transition patients back into their care.
