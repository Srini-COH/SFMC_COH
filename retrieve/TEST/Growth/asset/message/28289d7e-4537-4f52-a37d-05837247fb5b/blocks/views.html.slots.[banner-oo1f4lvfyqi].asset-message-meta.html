Using DNA sequencing to uncover more inherited cancer mutations

Genetic tests are all about assessing cancer risk by searching for inherited DNA mutations. Genomic tests have long been used to detect acquired mutations, which account for the vast majority of cancers. Now scientists are finding ways to use genomic tests to detect both inherited and acquired mutations, and the information is having an important impact, not only in expanding patients’ potential treatment options but in arming their family members with key health information.
