This week is Cancer Screen Week—a great time to review and schedule the tests that are right for you. Screenings are important for their ability to help detect cancer even if you're not experiencing any symptoms. Early detection is key.

Maintaining a consistent screening routine is critical. Use these 3 essential steps when making your plan:

  1. Know your family history with cancer.
  2. Tell your doctor about your cancer-related family history and any health concerns you may have.
  3. Ask your doctor which screenings are right for you and when you should get them.

Download the Cancer Screening Tracker from Cancer Treatment Centers of America® (CTCA) to help stay organized. This packet is helpful for keeping track of your health, family history and recommended screenings. It's also a great resource to share with loved ones to help encourage them to focus on their wellness and screening needs.
