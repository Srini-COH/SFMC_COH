Whether you're exploring the great outdoors or simply relaxing outside this summer, here are ways to minimize the risk that comes with sun exposure.

These 5 quick tips can help reduce your risk of developing skin cancer by decreasing your exposure to ultraviolet (UV) light:

What to do
1. Stay in the shade when the sun's rays are strongest, typically between 10 a.m. and 4 p.m.

What to wear
2. Sunglasses that block UV radiation
3. Long sleeves and pants if you’re in the sun
4. Wide-brimmed hat to protect your head and face
5. Broad-spectrum sunscreen that protects against UVA and UVB rays with a sun protection factor (SPF) of 30 or higher
