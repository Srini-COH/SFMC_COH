It's common for cancer patients, survivors and caregivers to feel stress. Reducing this feeling is both achievable and important.

Chronic stress has been associated with weakened immune function and increased susceptibility to infection, as well as worsening of conditions such as depression and heart disease.

You can use these tips to help manage, reduce and cope with stressors:

  • How stress affects your health: 10 tips for managing stress when you have cancer
  • Anxiety and stress in cancer patients: 6 tips for coping with stress
  • Behavioral health during cancer treatment: What is it? How does it help?

Even if you're not feeling stressed right now, learning about healthy coping strategies and support resources that are available to you may help in case you need it later in your journey.

At City of Hope, we offer cognitive behavioral therapies through our behavioral health services. Behavioral health, an integral part of whole-person care, recognizes the powerful ways emotional, mental, social and behavioral wellness may directly affect your physical health.
