Everybody gets anxious—often by the littlest things. But for anyone who may think they have cancer or learn that they're at a high risk of developing cancer, this uneasiness can be increased.

And while there's no definitive connection between stress and cancer, enough is understood about the association to suggest that being in a constant state of stress could be a risk factor.

Even if you're not feeling stressed right now, learning about healthy coping strategies and support resources that are available to you now may help if you need them later.
