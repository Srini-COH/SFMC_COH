Trends show that, while overall colorectal cancer cases and deaths have declined, diagnoses are increasing dramatically among younger adults.

The good news is colorectal cancer is one of the few that can be prevented, if precancerous polyps are found and removed before cancer has developed. The recommended age to start colorectal cancer screenings is 45 for those at average risk.

Learn more about colorectal cancer causes and risk factors, and to find out which factors can and cannot be controlled, consider these resources and tips:

  • Know the signs and symptoms.
  • Follow these screening guidelines, based on your age and risk factors.
    • Did you know there are different types of screenings?
      • Stool-based test at home
      • Virtual colonoscopy
      • Colonoscopy
  • Limit processed foods, and aim for a diet high in fiber, lean protein, fruits and vegetables to promote gut health.
  • Exercise, even in modest amounts, to help improve overall wellness.
