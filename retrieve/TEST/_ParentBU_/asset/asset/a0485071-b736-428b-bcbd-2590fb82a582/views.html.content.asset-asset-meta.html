Resources & support
 
 
During your evaluation at City of Hope, you will meet many cancer care experts. At City of Hope, you don't just have one specialist to help you fight cancer, you have a team of experts collaborating on your care.

The cancer care experts at City of Hope work together, all under one roof, collaborating with you to confirm your diagnosis and recommend a treatment plan tailored to your specific diagnosis, needs and goals. These experts will continually monitor your progress, making changes as necessary along the way.

Depending on your needs, your care team may include these experts:
  • Medical oncologist
  • Surgical oncologist
  • Radiation oncologist
  • Pain management physician
  • Rehabilitation therapist
  • Naturopathic oncology provider
  • Behavioral health provider

You may gain greater confidence in your treatment plan

If your evaluation confirms your original diagnosis, your City of Hope care team may provide additional treatment options for you to consider. However, if the evaluation uncovers a different type or stage of cancer, your care team may recommend a new treatment plan altogether.

Your care is coordinated under one roof

During your initial evaluation, and throughout treatment if you choose to treat at City of Hope, your doctor appointments, diagnostic tests and state-of-the-art cancer therapies are available at one location, with appointments coordinated for you on the same day or days, or whatever works best for your schedule.

We're committed to helping you handle these logistics, so you don't have to do it alone. That's why we developed a comprehensive care model that assigns a coordinator, or team of coordinators, to each patient's care team, to ensure that your medical needs are met in a seamless fashion. These coordinators are called a care managers, and they're dedicated to orchestrating your schedule, communication, education and follow-up. Some of the benefits of coordinated care include:

  • Convenience: Your care manager coordinates appointments for you.

  • One point of contact: Your care manager is available to answer your questions and concerns when you are at the hospital or at home.

  • Clear, timely communication: Whether it’s understanding medical jargon or knowing what to expect from treatments, your care manager helps you navigate your cancer journey.

Six reasons why a second opinion matters

 
 
1. Feel empowered and take control: By proactively seeking a second opinion, you and your loved ones may become more informed about available treatment options. Learning more about your cancer and your treatment options may help you feel in control of your health.

 
 
2. Gain greater peace of mind: A second opinion may help you feel more confident that you are choosing the right treatment plan. In some instances, a second opinion may uncover a different type or stage of cancer, which could change the treatment plan. If the original diagnosis is confirmed, a second opinion may provide additional treatment options for you to consider.
 
 
 
3. Discover advanced treatment options: Some hospitals have technology that is not available at another facility. Seeking a second opinion from a doctor in a health system that uses state-of-the art tools and technologies may provide more cancer treatment options, including treatments that are more advanced or more tailored to your individual needs.
 
 
 
4. Find a doctor you really like: Most doctors understand that patients have a right to a second opinion and should not feel offended. In fact, many doctors encourage it before making a treatment decision. When meeting with a new doctor, take note of the nurses and office staff to make sure you choose the team and hospital that fits your needs.
 
 
 
5. A greater chance of a correct diagnosis: If you’ve been diagnosed with a rare cancer, a second opinion may confirm the disease type and stage. If a pathologist has not encountered your type of cancer previously, there may be a greater chance of misdiagnosis.

 
 
6. A chance for hope: If one doctor says your cancer is untreatable, it is possible that another doctor may have access to other treatment options or clinical trials.
