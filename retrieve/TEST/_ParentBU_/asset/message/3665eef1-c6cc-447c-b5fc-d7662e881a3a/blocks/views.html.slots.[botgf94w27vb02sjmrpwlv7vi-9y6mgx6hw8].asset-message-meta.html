The summer sunshine brings a golden opportunity to spotlight important steps you can take to reduce the risk that comes with sun exposure.

Cancer patients and those who have survived cancers are at greater risk. Many treatments and medications may increase your sensitivity to direct sunlight.

These simple tips can help you protect yourself:

  • Avoid tanning salons and tanning beds.
  • Seek shade if you must be outside between 10 a.m. and 4 p.m., when the sun's ultraviolet (UV) light is typically at its strongest.
  • Perform regular skin self-examinations—here's how.
  • Use a broad-spectrum sunscreen that protects against UVA and UVB rays with a sun protection factor (SPF) of 30 or higher.
Use skin products wisely. Your doctor may advise you to avoid skin care products for at least four hours before treatment. You'll need to protect previously radiated skin from the sun even after radiation therapy is complete.

It's important to check your skin for new spots, irregular or raised spots or spots that have changed or grown. Examine yourself for changing blemishes at least once a month and be sure to check these 5 surprising spots where skin cancers may hide.
