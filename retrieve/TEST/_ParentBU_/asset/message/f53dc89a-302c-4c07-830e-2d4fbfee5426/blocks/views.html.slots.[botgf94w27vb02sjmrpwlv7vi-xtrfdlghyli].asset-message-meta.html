A consistent screening schedule, based on your doctor's recommendations, is critical to early cancer detection, which may help reduce cancer risk, including a cancer recurrence or second cancer.

Here are the three essential steps for cancer screenings:

  1. Know your family history with cancer.
  2. Tell your doctor about your cancer-related family history, in addition to any health concerns you may have.
  3. Ask your doctor which screenings are right for you and when to get them.
It's a good idea to ask questions and take notes when talking to your doctor about screenings. Use the Cancer Screening Tracker from Cancer Treatment Centers of America® (CTCA) to help collect and track pertinent information on your health, history and screenings.
