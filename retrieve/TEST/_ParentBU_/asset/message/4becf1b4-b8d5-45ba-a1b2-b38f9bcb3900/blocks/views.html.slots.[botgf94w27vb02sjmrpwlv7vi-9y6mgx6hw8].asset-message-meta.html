Cancer is stressful. It's common for cancer patients (and caregivers) to experience anxiety or struggle with mental health in some way. That's why it's important to learn coping strategies and to find support as soon as possible.

"Your mindset and attitude are important to your treatment experience during and after cancer treatment," says Karen R., a breast cancer patient at Cancer Treatment Centers of America® (CTCA).

Consider these resources to identify and help when you're emotionally out of sorts:

  • How do I know if I'm depressed?
  • Six tips for coping with stress
  • Ways to manage depression
  • From a patient: Six tips for mental health during treatment
The behavioral health program at CTCA® was established to support patients before, during and after cancer treatment. We offer a variety of psychosocial support services, from couples and one-on-one counseling to guided imagery and relaxation techniques, designed to help improve your emotional health and mental well-being. Know that you don't have to face it alone.
