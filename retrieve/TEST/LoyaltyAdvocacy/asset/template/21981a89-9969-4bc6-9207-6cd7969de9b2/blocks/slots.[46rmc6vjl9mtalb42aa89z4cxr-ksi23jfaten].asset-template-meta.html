As you know, lung cancer is the leading cause of cancer deaths in the US. However, when diagnosed at stage IA (nodule 3cm or less in size), the average 5-year survival rate is about 80%.1 The overall survival improves as nodules are identified at an even smaller size (92% when the nodule is <1cm in size). Despite slow overall adaptation, with the availability of low-dose CT scan for lung cancer screening, many small peripheral lung nodules are being found. The current diagnostic yield of peripheral lung nodules less than 3cm in diameter, with both bronchoscopic and transthoracic (CT-guided) biopsies, is less than 80 percent. For lesions that are less than 1cm in size, the diagnostic yield is far lower.

The Ion endoluminal system by Intuitive, maker of the da Vinci surgical system, is an advancement in the existing approaches to lung biopsy. The Ion system allows for a high rate of diagnostic yield, especially in the nodules that are <2cm in size. Also, if found to be malignant, endobronchial ultrasound (EBUS) for staging can be performed at the same setting. The rate of pneumothorax is much less than CT-guided biopsy.

CTCA Phoenix is using the Ion endoluminal system because it is designed to provide more reach, more stability, and more precision compared to flexible bronchoscopy with or without electromagnetic navigation (EMN).2
