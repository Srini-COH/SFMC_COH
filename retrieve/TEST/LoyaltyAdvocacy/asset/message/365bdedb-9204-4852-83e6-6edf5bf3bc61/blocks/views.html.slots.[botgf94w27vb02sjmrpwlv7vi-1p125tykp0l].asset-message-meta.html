This month being National Colorectal Cancer Awareness month, a much-needed spotlight is cast on the importance of early detection. Colorectal cancer is the fourth most common cancer in the United States and, according to the American Cancer Society, an estimated 151,030 people will be diagnosed this year. If caught before it spreads to other parts of the body, the five-year survival rate increases to 90 percent. Check out the resources below on screening guidelines, symptoms and more.
