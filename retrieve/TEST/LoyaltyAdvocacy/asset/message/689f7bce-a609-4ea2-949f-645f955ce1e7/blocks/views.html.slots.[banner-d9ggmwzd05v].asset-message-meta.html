Early Colorectal Screening and Detection Starts Younger
 
This year at your annual physical, don't be surprised if your doctor suggests a colonoscopy, even if you're still in your 40s. "The increasing incidence of colorectal cancer in adults in their 20s and 30s is alarming to many of us," says Eyal Meiri, MD, Interim Chief of Medical Oncology at Cancer Treatment Centers of America® (CTCA), Atlanta. For colorectal cancer survivor Scott D., symptoms appeared at the age of 49. Read on for the new screening guidelines and Scott's insights.
