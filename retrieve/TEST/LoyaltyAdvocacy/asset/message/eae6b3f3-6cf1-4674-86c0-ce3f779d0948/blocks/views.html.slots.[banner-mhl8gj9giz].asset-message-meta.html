Every week, your congregation comes to church seeking a deeper relationship with Christ. This personal relationship provides each person with a unique foundation that keeps them strong if and when facing a strenuous cancer journey.
 
We know that faith can make a big difference in someone's life, and that includes their perspective on health and medical care. According to the “Coping with Cancer” study by Dana-Farber Cancer Institute and Harvard Medical School:
  • 88 percent of patients considered religion to be at least somewhat important.*
  • Spiritual support was strongly linked with a better quality of life.*
  • But 47 percent said their spiritual needs were supported minimally or not at all by a religious community.*
Your cancer care ministry is needed! To read more about the faith factor and how your small group participants can help promote health and well-being among your congregation, visit the Ministry Resources section of our website.
 
Once you have determined your start date for your small group training, please call or email me so I can send you additional follow-up materials. If you have any further questions or concerns, please feel free to contact me. I am happy to help. Thank you for committing to provide cancer care ministry.
