Every week, your congregation comes to church seeking a deeper relationship with Christ. This personal relationship provides each person with a unique foundation that keeps them strong through life's challenges, especially when facing a cancer journey.

We know that faith can make a big difference in someone's life, and that includes their perspective on health and medical care.

According to a cancer study by Dana-Farber Cancer Institute* and Harvard Medical School, 88% of patients considered religion to be at least somewhat important, but, 47% said their spiritual needs were supported minimally or not at all by a religious community.

This is why your cancer care ministry is needed!

Read more about the faith factor and how your small group participants can help promote health and well-being among your congregation. For more information, log in and check out the Ministry Resources online.
