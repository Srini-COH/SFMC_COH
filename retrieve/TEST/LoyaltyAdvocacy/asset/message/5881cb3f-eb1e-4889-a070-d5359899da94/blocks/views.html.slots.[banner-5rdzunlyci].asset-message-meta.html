Dear %%=v(@FirstName)=%%,

As you may already know, current state law restricts Georgians from choosing Cancer Treatment Centers of America® (CTCA) for cancer care because we were defined by law in 2008 as a "destination cancer hospital,” restricted to 50 beds, and only allowed to see 35% of our patients from Georgia. We currently have legislation both in the Senate and the House that contains language to address this issue by removing those restrictions and allowing CTCA® to operate as a General Hospital focused solely on adult cancer care under the same Certificate of Need rules that govern other hospitals.
 
Your Senator, Jack Hill, is on the Senate Regulated Industries & Utilities Committee. This committee will ultimately decide whether Senate Bill 74 will make it through to the Senate and House floors for a vote.
