Calling all Cancer Fighters®,

Thank you for taking quick action to contact members of the House Health & Human Services Committee in support of a substitute to Senate Bill 31. Your calls and emails flooded the Georgia State Capitol, making an immediate and significant impact in last week’s committee hearing.

Several Committee Members, including Chairman Sharon Cooper (R-Marietta) and Rep. Allen Peake (R-Macon), Rep. Matt Hatchett (R-Dublin), Rep. Jodi Lott (R-Evans), Rep. Scott Hilton (R-Peachtree Corners), Rep. Tim Barr (R-Lawrenceville), Rep. John Pezold (R-Columbus), Rep. Betty Price (R-Roswell) and Rep. Karen Mathiak (R-Griffin), voiced their support for the Senate Bill 31 substitute, which would grant Georgia citizens the full opportunity to choose where they receive care in the state. We highlight and thank Sen. Matt Brass (R-Newnan) for introducing the original legislation earlier in Session and courageously staying the course. 

While we did not receive the votes needed in the House Health & Human Services Committee to pass the Senate Bill 31 substitute, the movement to give choice back to Georgia's cancer patients is still alive. A motion for bill reconsideration was granted, meaning our fight for patient choice is not over.

Speak Now Georgia will keep you updated on further progress, but in the meantime, please continue to contact your state legislators and demand reform for cancer patients. It’s easy… visit http://speaknowgeorgia.org/contact-your-legislators/.


Cancer doesn't wait. Neither should you. 

Sincerely,
Speak Now Georgia
